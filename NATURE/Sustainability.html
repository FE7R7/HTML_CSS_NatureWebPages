SUSTAINABILITY

Click on the image above to be redirected to an interesting video content produced by Trinity College Dublin.

Sustainability is the ability to enjoy the natural resources present on the planet without compromising their use for future generations.
In this sense, for example, we have more and more applicability of the term in everyday actions such as the consumption of natural products, reuse of packaging, recycling, use of less polluting transport.
Investing in measures aimed at applying sustainable practices in the production chain are actions that several companies are adopting to improve processes, to reduce the environmental impact and to attract consumers.


Environmental sustainability relates to the carrying capacity, resilience and resistance of ecosystems. It can also be defined as an evaluation process between the economy, society and nature.
It is this type of sustainability that defines the way in which human beings use goods and natural resources to meet their own needs, so that they are not depleted, with guarantees for the next generations.

Check out examples:

  1. Use of clean and renewable energy sources

  2. Rationalization and control over the exploitation of mineral resources

  3. Conscious exploration and controlled management of natural resources

  4. Reforestation and enhancement of green areas

  5. Selective collection and recycling of waste

  6. Rationalization of water

  7. Reduction of deforestation and the practice of burning

  8. Creation of environmental protection areas and carrying out due inspection

  9. Promotion of reforestation actions
